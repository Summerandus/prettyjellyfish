<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">

There is a particular quality of light at the boundary between two bodies of water — where the river meets the sea, where the warm current meets the cold. Neither one thing nor another. Both, and more than both.

I have lived most of my life at thresholds like this.

Between languages, where the word I need exists in one but not the other, and so I carry both in my mouth until I find somewhere to set one down. Between cities that do not quite claim me. Between the precision of systems and the softness of what cannot be measured.

---

For a long time I thought this was a problem to solve. That eventually I would arrive somewhere and the between-ness would stop.

I don't think that anymore.

The jellyfish has no spine. It moves by contraction and release, by reading the water around it, by being willing to drift. It does not navigate. It simply responds — with its whole translucent body — to the medium it moves through.

I am learning to trust the drift.

</div>

<div class="lang-zh" markdown="1">

在两个水体的边界处，有一种特殊的光质——河流与大海的交汇，暖流与冷流的相遇。既非此，亦非彼。两者皆是，又超越两者。

我的大半生都生活在这样的边界上。

在两种语言之间，我需要的那个词只存在于其中一种，于是我将两者都含在口中，直到找到一个地方放下其中一个。在两座都不完全属于我的城市之间。在系统的精确与无法被衡量的柔软之间。

---

很长一段时间里，我以为这是一个需要解决的问题。以为终有一天我会抵达某处，这种"之间感"便会停止。

我不再这么想了。

水母没有脊椎。它靠收缩与舒张移动，靠感知周围的水，靠愿意漂流。它不导航。它只是用整个半透明的身体，回应它所穿行的介质。

我正在学习信任漂流。

</div>
